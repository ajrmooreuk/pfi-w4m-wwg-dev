## Executive Summary

FAIRSLICE-ONT v1.0.0 and PARTNER-ONT v1.0.0 are complete and OAA-compliant. The ontology layer defines **what** FairSlice is. This briefing defines **how** — the VE Skill Chain methodology for bringing team members and external partners into the elaboration and implementation of FairSlice as a live platform capability.

The core insight: **FairSlice is both the product AND the method by which it is built.** The people who elaborate the methodology (team, partners) are the same people who will operate within its economic model (builders, rainmakers, architects, partners). The VE Skill Chain governs this dual nature — strategic intent flows down through VSOM → BSC → OKR → KPI → VP → RRR, while contribution data flows back up through Slices → Claims → Distributions.

This proposal structures the collaboration as a **PFC-PFI cascade** where:
- **PFC-Core** owns the FairSlice methodology, ontologies, and base smart contracts
- **PFI Instances** customise multipliers, waterfall rules, and partner terms per domain
- **EMC + Unified Registry** govern composition, promotion, and artifact lifecycle
- **ORG-ONT + PARTNER-ONT** define who participates and how parties impact the model
- **RRR-ONT** assigns accountability through RACI across every collaboration touchpoint

The VE Skill Chain defines **seven collaboration stages** — each with specific participants, governance, and outputs.

| Stage | VE Skill | Activity | Participants | Governance | Output |

## 2. Parties, Individuals & Their Impact on the Model

### 2.1 Party Model (ORG-ONT + PARTNER-ONT + RRR-ONT)

Every participant in the FairSlice elaboration maps to three ontological layers:

```
ORG-ONT (Identity Layer)
  WHO they are — legal entity, org type, structure
     │
     ▼
PARTNER-ONT / RRR-ONT (Role Layer)
  WHAT they do — partner tier, functional role, RACI assignment
     │
     ▼
FAIRSLICE-ONT (Economic Layer)
  HOW they are rewarded — member role, multiplier, waterfall position
```

### 2.2 Party Classification

| Party Type | ORG-ONT Entity | PARTNER-ONT Entity | RRR-ONT Role | FAIRSLICE-ONT Role | Impact on Model |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|-------------|-------------------|----------------|

| **Strategic Partner** | org:Organization (type: Partner) | partner:Partner (tier: Gold/Platinum) | rrr:StrategicPartner | PieMember (Architect) + Partner (Agency) | Co-create smart contracts, validate VP, influence waterfall structure |

| **Affiliate** | org:Organization OR Individual | partner:Affiliate | rrr:Referrer | Partner (Affiliate) | Referral attribution, platform fee share, expand reach |
| **Architect** | Individual or org:Organization | — | rrr:DomainExpert | PieMember (Architect) | Publish smart contracts, earn royalties, grow marketplace |
| **Builder** | Individual | — | rrr:Engineer | PieMember (Builder) | Ship code/content, earn slices with 2x multiplier |

| **Advisor** | Individual | — | rrr:Advisor | PieMember (Advisor) | Strategic guidance, earn slices with 0.5x multiplier |

### 2.3 Individual Impact Mechanics

Every individual's participation impacts the model through **four feedback channels**:

```
CONTRIBUTION CHANNEL (bottom-up)
  Individual submits Claim → AI Judge evaluates → Slices allocated
  Impact: ownership % recalculates dynamically
  Governance: Smart Contract rules (via Unified Registry)

REVENUE CHANNEL (top-down)
  Revenue Event arrives → Waterfall executes → Distributions flow
  Impact: individual receives payout proportional to role + ownership
  Governance: WaterfallRule priority order (BR-FS-001 to BR-FS-012)

GOVERNANCE CHANNEL (lateral)
  Individual holds RRR role → RACI assignment → accountability scope
  Impact: decisions, approvals, escalations follow role hierarchy
  Governance: GRC-FW audit trail (append-only)

STRATEGY CHANNEL (cyclical)
  Individual OKR progress → KPI measurement → BSC review → VSOM adjustment
  Impact: quarterly strategy pivots based on actual contribution data
  Governance: VSOM-ONT + OKR-ONT + KPI-ONT cascade
```

Partners affect the model differently depending on their tier and type. This matrix governs how partner participation shapes the FairSlice economic model:

|---------------|-------------|----------------|----------------|

| Agency manages a pie | Management fee injected into waterfall (priority 3) | JP-FS-003: waterfall→partner | Agency RLS isolation |

| Partner disputes commission | Escalation to human review; audit trail consulted | GRC-FW: GovernanceAssurance | Immutable audit (BR-FS-004) |

## 3. PFC-PFI Cascade: Governance Architecture

### 3.1 EMC Composition for FairSlice

EMC-ONT orchestrates which ontologies activate at each scope level. FairSlice introduces a new EMC category:

```
EMC CATEGORY: ECONOMICS (New — extends the 11 existing categories)

  PFC-Core Composition:
    Required:  FAIRSLICE-ONT, PARTNER-ONT, RRR-ONT, GRC-FW-ONT
    Recommended: VSOM-ONT, KPI-ONT, BSC-ONT, VP-ONT
    Optional:  OKR-ONT, ORG-CONTEXT-ONT

  PFI Instance Filtering:
    If PFI.instanceOntologies includes "FAIRSLICE" →
      Activate ECONOMICS category with instance overrides
    Else →
      ECONOMICS category not available (no FairSlice capability)

  Cascade Resolution:
    PFC defaults (base multipliers, waterfall, contracts)
      → PFI override (domain-specific roles, commission rates)
        → Pie override (venture-specific custom terms)
```

### 3.2 Unified Registry Integration

FairSlice artifacts follow the same registry lifecycle as all PFC artifacts:

| Artifact Type | Registry Entry | Cascade Behaviour | Promotion Path |
|--------------|---------------|-------------------|---------------|
| `fairslice-defaults` | Base multipliers, waterfall config | Core → Instance → Pie | Registry review cadence |
| `smart-contract` | AI Judge system prompts, validation rules | Core → Instance (immutable after publish) | Architect publishes → Registry gate → Active |
| `partner-programme` | Tier thresholds, commission templates | Core → Instance | Quarterly review |
| `waterfall-template` | Priority-ordered step definitions | Core → Instance → Pie | Instance admin approval |
| `claim-template` | Claim types, proof requirements | Core → Instance | Domain-specific customisation |

**Resolution function:** `resolve_cascaded_config()` — same function serves both FairSlice pies and all other registry artifacts. One function, many consumers.

### 4.2 Phase 1: Strategic Alignment with Partners (Weeks 1-4)

#### Step 1.1: Partner Identification (via ORG-ONT + ORG-CONTEXT-ONT)

```

  1. Create org:Organization entry (type, size, market context)
  2. Create org-context:OrganizationContext (products, competitive landscape)
  3. Assess partner:PartnerTier eligibility (Bronze → Platinum criteria)

**Partner Selection Criteria (Three Voices — aligned with Epic 55 PPM):**

```

  Instance: PFC-Core (not PFI-specific — this is the platform itself)
  Ontology Config: FAIRSLICE-ONT base defaults

  MEMBERS:
    AJM          → Founder/Builder  (2.0x multiplier, equity)
    [Builder 2]  → Builder          (2.0x multiplier, equity)
    [Rainmaker]  → Rainmaker        (1.0x multiplier, commission)
    [Partner A]  → Architect        (1.5x multiplier, royalties via smart contracts)
    [Advisor]    → Advisor          (0.5x multiplier, equity)

  WATERFALL (inherited from PFC defaults):
    1. Platform Fee:      5%  → PFC-Core operations
    2. Affiliate Payout:  10% of platform fee → if referral exists
    3. Agency Retainer:   0%  → no agency on Pie Zero
    4. Architect Royalty:  per-license fee → to Partner A for smart contracts
    5. OpEx Recovery:     tracked → reinvested
    6. Tax Reserve:       calculated → HMRC
    7. Dividend Pool:     remainder → by ownership %

  CLAIMS:
    Builder ships F50.3 (Supabase schema) → Claim → AI Judge → 40 base * 2.0 = 80 slices
    Builder ships F50.5 (Waterfall engine) → Claim → AI Judge → 60 base * 2.0 = 120 slices
    Rainmaker signs Partner A → Claim → AI Judge → 30 base * 1.0 = 30 slices
    Architect publishes smart contract → Claim → AI Judge → 25 base * 1.5 = 37.5 slices

**This is the self-referential validation:** if the FairSlice model is fair, the team building it should be willing to use it on themselves. If not, the model needs adjustment before launch.

## 10. Summary: The VE Skill Chain as Collaboration Governance

The FairSlice elaboration follows the VE Skill Chain not as a metaphor but as a **literal governance structure**:

1. **VSOM** defines what FairSlice is (done) and how it evolves (this briefing)
2. **BSC** sets measurable targets from 5 perspectives (done in Epic 50)
3. **OKR** decomposes targets into individual assignments via **RRR roles** (Phase 2)
4. **KPI** tracks leading and lagging indicators for every participant (Phase 2-3)
5. **VP** validates value propositions with the actual target segments — **partners are both validators and participants** (Phase 1)
6. **RRR** assigns accountability for every collaboration touchpoint — who approves, who builds, who sells
7. **EMC** governs which ontologies activate at each scope level — preventing capability sprawl
8. **Unified Registry** manages artifact lifecycle — smart contracts, templates, and partner programmes all follow the same cascade
9. **ORG-ONT + PARTNER-ONT** define who participates and how their participation impacts the economic model
10. **GRC-FW-ONT** ensures every decision, distribution, and claim is immutably audited

**The result:** A methodology where the people building FairSlice are governed by FairSlice's own principles — contribution-based, transparent, auditable, and fair. When this works internally, it's ready for external partners. When partners validate it, it's ready for PFI instances. When instances customise it, it's ready for 100 clients.
